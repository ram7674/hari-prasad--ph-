About Us

Group Chairman & Non-executive Director

Born into a medical family (father is a doctor who has been operating a 150 bedded hospital for the last 40 years) a graduate degree in medicine was a natural outcome. Having graduated the search for specialization began and the pull was towards the glamour specialties like orthopedics and cardiac surgery. However not wanting to get restricted to one specialty and wanting to contribute over a larger area, Anesthesiology became the specialty of choice. Having specialized in anesthesiology, Dr. Prasad worked in at the family hospital for 4 years before embarking on a journey to contribute to the establishment of emergency medicine as a specialty India. At that point of time there was no concept of emergency medicine in the country and few believed in the benefits of the new specialty.

Experience

1992 to 1997

Consultant, Anesthesiology & Critic al Care - Sridevi Hospital, Hyderabad.

This is a 150 bedded multi-specialty Hospital established in 1972. Apart from providing clinical anaesthesia services to all major services, expanded the scope of services of the department by establishing a 6 bedded Critical Care Unit and introducing Pain Management services including Labor Analgesia. Actively contributed to the growth of this hospital from primary care obstetrics dominated facility into a higher secondary care facility with a multi-specialty focus.

1997 to 1999

Consultant, Anaesthesiology & Critical Care — Apollo Emergency Hospital, Hyderabad

Dr. Prasad played a key role in establishing Apollo Emergency Hospital in Hyderabad, India's first dedicated accident and emergency hospital, pioneering emergency medicine in the country. Involved from inception, he set up a comprehensive emergency room and deployed fully equipped ambulances. Despite a shortage of trained personnel, Dr. Prasad and colleagues managed to transport critically ill patients and run the emergency room. Their dedication quickly made Apollo Emergency Hospital the preferred destination for emergencies.

1999 to 2001

Coordinator, Emergency Services, Apollo Hospitals, Hyderabad.

Dr. Prasad, after successfully implementing the emergency care model at Apollo Emergency Hospital, extended the concept to two other locations in Hyderabad, forming a network of three emergency rooms and eight ambulances. Despite initial skepticism from other hospitals, Dr. Prasad's persistence led to the creation of "emergency clinics," endorsed by Dr. Pratap C Reddy, resulting in the establishment of five additional emergency rooms, totaling 20 ambulances citywide. This pioneering effort birthed India's first comprehensive emergency network, serving as a model for the nation and underscoring the significance of accessible emergency medical services in communities.

2001 to 2004

Director Emergency Services, Apollo Group of Hospitals

The model established in Hyderabad was fine-tuned and Dr. Prasad was given the job of replicating it across the Apollo Group of Hospitals in India. Dr.Prasad worked on establishing the model cross different geographies and successfully implemented it in Chennai, Delhi, Kolkatta, Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Mysore, Pune Madurai, Kakinada etc. The network today operates about 150 ambulances and 60 emergency rooms in the year 2002 Dr.Prasad was instrumental in launching air ambulance services for the group. Apollo Group today does the highest number of air ambulances transfers in the country.

2003 to 2006

Vice President- Medical Services, Apollo Hospitals, Hyderabad.

Dr. Prasad oversaw operations, medical services, and business development across various Apollo facilities in Hyderabad, spanning tertiary, secondary, and emergency care. He integrated these units into a pioneering hub-and-spoke healthcare model, elevating performance both individually and collectively. Notably, this marked the first such model in the private sector nationally. Under his leadership, facilities like Apollo Hospital in Secunderabad and DRDO were upgraded to tertiary care, supported by a dedicated team focused on process re-engineering. Dr. Prasad navigated Hyderabad's competitive healthcare landscape by emphasizing service quality and premium tariffs, effectively positioning Apollo Hospitals as a distinct leader known for superior care and outcomes.

2006 to 2010

Chief Executive Officer, Apollo Hospitals, Hyderabad.

As CEO of Apollo Hospitals in Hyderabad, Dr. Prasad oversaw a diverse range of healthcare facilities, including a 550-bed quaternary care hospital in Jubilee Hills and a 175-bed A&E hospital in Hyderguda. He also managed tertiary care hospitals in Secunderabad and DRDO, along with emergency clinics in Kukatpally, Malakpet, and Mehdipatnam. Additionally, Dr. Prasad was responsible for tertiary care hospitals in Kakinada and Karimnagar, as well as a medical center at Shamshabad Airport and a PET-CT center.

2010 to 2015

Chief Executive Officer — Central Region of Apollo Hospitals, Group

Dr. Prasad oversaw hospitals in multiple states, pioneering innovative programs and achieving significant milestones such as Joint Commission International Accreditation. He successfully transformed a struggling hospital in Kakinada into a profitable institution and established Apollo Reach Hospitals in Karimnagar. Dr. Prasad's vision also led to the development of Asia's first functional Health City in Jubilee Hills. Under his leadership, Apollo Hospitals, Hyderabad consistently achieved double-digit revenue and EBIDTA growth, earning recognition as the best hospital in the region and nationwide acclaim for service excellence and clinical outcomes benchmarking.

2015 - 2024

Group President for Apollo Hospitals Division.

Dr. Prasad spearheaded standardization efforts across Apollo Hospitals, implementing SOPs and automation to enhance efficiency and stakeholder value. This approach significantly improved patient satisfaction, revenue cycle management, and employee morale. Dr. Prasad fostered an adaptable organizational culture, embracing digitalization and performance metrics to ensure readiness for the future. During the pandemic, unified protocols across 74 hospitals ensured effective healthcare delivery, showcasing the system's resilience.

April 2024 onwards

Group Chairman & Non-executive Director

Quality Care India Ltd. (Care Group of Hospitals, KIMSHEALTH Group of Hospitals in Kerala & Evercare Group of Hospitals in Bangladesh)
